Clinical Trial Phases

Clinical trials are conducted in phases, each with a specific purpose and set of objectives. The phases are:

  • Phase 1: Tests the safety and dosage of a drug.
  • Phase 2: Evaluates the effectiveness and side effects.
  • Phase 3: Confirms effectiveness, monitors side effects, and compares to commonly used treatments.
  • Phase 4: Post-marketing studies to gather additional information.
